Data

Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates of Child and Parent Income Distributions by College

Data provided by Opportunity Insights

Dataset license

Accessed in Spring 2020

The original dataset contains 35 columns and around 2k rows.

The columns I've used:

  1. Female to Male Ratio: This column describes the fraction of females among students in a given row.
  2. School Tier: This column describes the selectivity and type of a school.
  3. Parental Mean Income:The mean income of the parents of the students attending the school.
  4. Kid Mean Income:The mean income of the students having attended the school.

Wrangling

I made a Python script that picks 100 rows out of the dataset at random to visualize.

Parallel Line Chart in D3 v5

Here each line represents a school from the dataset. I've limited myself to 100 lines since overcrowding the graph makes it illegible.

A conclusion we can make looking at this visualization is that kids with parnts on the lower end of the income bracket don't really have access to the top tier schools, those kids also end up making less than the ones whos parents make more money.

We can also see that most schools in this subset have a balanced Male to Female ratio.

School Tier: 1 = Ivy Plus, 2 = Other elite schools (public and private), 3 = Highly selective public, 4 = Highly selective private, 5 = Selective public, 6 = Selective private, 7 = Nonselective 4-year public, 8 = Nonselective 4-year private not-for-profit, 9 = Two-year (public and private not-for-profit), 10 = Four-year for-profit, 11 = Two-year for-profit, 12 = Less than two year schools of any type 13 = Attending college with insufficient data, 14 = Not in college between the ages of 19-22

Data source: Opportunity Insights, Author: Nedim Azar
